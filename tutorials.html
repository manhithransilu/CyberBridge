Cyber Basics Learning Path

Welcome to the CyberBridge Learning Hub! This structured learning path introduces foundational cybersecurity concepts in a beginner-friendly sequence. Each module builds upon the previous one, taking you from understanding how the internet works to implementing advanced protection strategies.

Estimated completion time: 2-3 hours total
Prerequisites: Basic computer literacy
Target audience: University students, faculty, and staff

Video: Cybersecurity for Beginners

Learning Modules

Module 1

How the Web Works

Before you can protect yourself online, you need to understand how the internet actually works. This module explains the journey data takes from your browser to a website and back, introducing key concepts like IP addresses, DNS, and HTTP/HTTPS protocols.

Understanding these fundamentals helps you recognise when something suspicious is happening with your connection, such as man-in-the-middle attacks or unsecured connections.

Key Learning Points:

  • How browsers communicate with web servers
  • The role of IP addresses and DNS in internet connectivity
  • Difference between HTTP and HTTPS (secure connections)
  • How data packets travel across networks
  • Introduction to encryption in transit
Module 2

Identifying Everyday Threats

Cyber threats aren't just for large corporations—students are frequent targets. This module helps you recognise the most common threats you'll encounter, from suspicious emails to malicious downloads, with real-world examples relevant to university life.

You'll learn to identify the warning signs of phishing attempts, understand how malware spreads, and recognise social engineering tactics that attackers use to manipulate victims.

Key Learning Points:

  • Types of phishing: email, SMS (smishing), and voice (vishing)
  • Recognising suspicious email characteristics
  • Understanding malware types: viruses, worms, trojans, ransomware
  • Social engineering tactics and psychological manipulation
  • Fake websites and how to spot them
  • Common student-targeted scams (fake scholarships, job offers)
Module 3

Protecting Your Digital Identity

Your digital identity is valuable—both to you and to cybercriminals. This module covers strategies for protecting your personal information online, from creating strong passwords to understanding what data you're sharing and with whom.

You'll learn about identity theft risks, how to monitor your digital footprint, and practical steps to secure your online accounts against unauthorised access.

Key Learning Points:

  • Creating strong, memorable passphrases
  • Using password managers effectively
  • Setting up and using multi-factor authentication (MFA)
  • Understanding what personal data you share online
  • Privacy settings on common platforms
  • Recognising and preventing identity theft
  • Checking if your accounts have been compromised
Module 4

Safe Social Media Practices

Social media is an essential part of student life, but it comes with significant security and privacy risks. This module teaches you how to enjoy social platforms while minimising your exposure to threats and protecting your personal information.

From understanding what information is visible to others to recognising social media-based attacks, you'll gain the skills to navigate these platforms safely.

Key Learning Points:

  • Privacy settings on Instagram, Facebook, Twitter/X, and LinkedIn
  • Understanding who can see your posts and profile
  • Risks of oversharing (location, schedule, personal details)
  • Recognising fake profiles and impersonation attempts
  • Dealing with cyberbullying and harassment
  • Digital reputation management for future employment
  • Third-party app permissions and data access
Module 5

Campus Network Security

University campuses present unique cybersecurity challenges. Shared computers, public Wi-Fi networks, and collaborative online environments require special precautions. This module focuses on staying secure specifically within the university context.

You'll learn best practices for using campus resources safely, protecting your work on shared devices, and securing your connection when working remotely.

Key Learning Points:

  • Safely using campus Wi-Fi (eduroam and guest networks)
  • Protecting your session on shared library computers
  • Secure use of learning management systems (Blackboard, Canvas)
  • VPN usage for off-campus access to university resources
  • Secure file sharing and collaboration
  • Protecting research data and academic work
  • University IT policies and why they matter

Continue Your Learning

After completing these modules, reinforce your knowledge by:

  • Reviewing the Core 4 Protective Habits
  • Exploring our Resource Library for advanced topics
  • Testing your knowledge with our practical exercises
  • Sharing what you've learned with fellow students

Remember: Cybersecurity is not a one-time task but an ongoing practice. Stay curious, stay vigilant, and stay safe online!
